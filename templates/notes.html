Social - World War 1 - Notes

Synopsis

World War 1 was a global conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918. It was fought between the Allied Powers, which included France, Russia, Britain, Italy, and the United States, and the Central Powers, which included Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria, and the Ottoman Empire. The war was marked by trench warfare and the use of new technologies such as tanks and machine guns.

Important Points

  • World War 1 was fought between the Allied Powers and the Central Powers from 1914 to 1918.
  • The main causes of the war were the rise of nationalism, imperialism, and militarism in Europe.
  • The war was fought mainly in Europe, but also in Africa and the Middle East.
  • The war was marked by trench warfare and the use of new technologies such as tanks and machine guns.
  • The war had a devastating impact on Europe, with millions of casualties and economic damage.
  • The war ended with the signing of the Treaty of Versailles in 1919.

Definitions

  • Allied Powers: The countries that fought against the Central Powers in World War 1, including France, Russia, Britain, Italy, and the United States.
  • Central Powers: The countries that fought against the Allied Powers in World War 1, including Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria, and the Ottoman Empire.
  • Nationalism: The belief that a nation should be independent and have its own government.
  • Imperialism: The policy of extending a nation's power and influence by acquiring other territories.
  • Militarism: The policy of maintaining a large military force and using it aggressively to gain power and influence.
  • Trench Warfare: A type of warfare in which soldiers fight from trenches dug in the ground.
