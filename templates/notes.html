Electromagnetic Repulsion - Notes

Synopsis

Electromagnetic repulsion is a force between two objects that have the same electric charge. This force is caused by the electromagnetic fields of the two objects repelling each other.

Important Points

  • The force of electromagnetic repulsion is proportional to the product of the charges of the two objects.
  • The force of electromagnetic repulsion is inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the two objects.
  • The force of electromagnetic repulsion is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude and direction.
  • The force of electromagnetic repulsion can be calculated using Coulomb’s law: \(F = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2}\), where k is the Coulomb’s constant, q1 and q2 are the charges of the two objects, and r is the distance between them.

Definitions

  • Electromagnetic Repulsion: A force between two objects that have the same electric charge.
  • Coulomb’s Law: The law used to calculate the force of electromagnetic repulsion between two objects.

Formulas

Coulomb’s Law: \(F = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2}\)

Example Problems

Problem 1: Find the force of electromagnetic repulsion between two objects with charges of +3C and -2C, separated by a distance of 5m.

Solution: Using Coulomb’s law:

\(F = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2} = \frac{9 \times 10^9 \times 3 \times (-2)}{(5)^2} = -2.16 \times 10^8 \text{ N}\)

Problem 2: Determine the distance between two objects with charges of -4C and +6C if the force of electromagnetic repulsion between them is 8.64 N.

Solution: Using Coulomb’s law:

\(F = \frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2} \Rightarrow r = \sqrt{\frac{kq_1q_2}{F}} = \sqrt{\frac{9 \times 10^9 \times (-4) \times 6}{8.64}} = 4.2 \text{ m}\)
